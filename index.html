Australian Road Fatalities

1989 - 2023

Each year, the Australian Government records the number of road-related fatalities, including deaths involving drivers, passengers, pedestrians, motorcyclists, and oversized vehicles, such as buses and heavy rigid trucks.

The following data is collected from the Australian Road Deaths Database, published by the Department of Infrastructure, Regional Development, and Cities. The database was last updated in August 2023.

Number of fatalities by day and time (1989 - 2023)

Road deaths typically occur in the mid‐afternoon, which is the period of time associated with increased road occupancy.

The road use associated with the greatest risk of death is driving, followed by using the road as a passenger. The age group with the greatest number of deaths is young adults (17 to 25 years of age).
